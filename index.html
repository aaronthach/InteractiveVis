Our World is Dying (Scroll through poem to see data)
By Aaron Thach
  • The wind blows,
  • caressing the birds perched atop the trees.
  • The ocean flows, harmoniously dancing
  • with the beautiful coral of the sea.
  • A powerful polar bear rests on sea ice
  • sleepily watching its cubs play.
  • An owl swoops down from the sky to catch mice
  • and feed its owlets before day.
  • However, times are changing.
  • The beauty that once was is dying.
  • The earth's resources are quickly draining.
  • Mother nature sits, weakly crying.
  • The world is warmer than it once was.
  • Disasters are more frequent than they were.
  • Our world is dying and we are the cause.
  • We’re to blame for putting profit before her.
  • Atmospheric carbon dioxide has increased
  • as the forests holding them have diminished.
  • We’re a savage beast,
  • destroying our world and the creatures in it.
  • The vicious hurricane rages,
  • forcing the innocent birds away from their homes.
  • The globe is hotter than it's been in ages.
  • The once robust coral will soon be dead as stone.
  • The sea ice melts, forcing the polar bears on land.
  • They roam around, encounter humans and attack.
  • Disease strikes the mice population and
  • a flood shatters the owl’s nest as the sky turns black.
